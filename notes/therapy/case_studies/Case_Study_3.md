# **Case Study 3: ISS + V.I.T.A.L. Applied to a Collapse Structure**  
*A therapist works with a client experiencing sudden shutdowns under pressure.*

---

## **Client Snapshot**
**Client:** “Elena,” 45, nonprofit director  
**Presenting Issue:** Episodes of emotional and cognitive shutdown during high-stakes meetings  
**Underlying Structure:** Collapse — rapid loss of agency, perspective, and internal coherence  
**Therapeutic Goal:** Increase structural awareness, identify collapse triggers, restore pre‑collapse agency

---

# **Part 1 — ISS in Action**

## **1. ISS Entry Point**
Therapist:

> “What feels most alive or charged for you right now?”

**Client Response:**  
“I freeze in meetings. I go from confident to blank in seconds. It’s like everything inside me drops.”

**Clinician Note:**  
The “alive” material is the *drop* — the collapse — not the meetings themselves.

---

## **2. Surface the Structure**
Therapist:

> “If you look at this as a structure, what shape does it have?”

**Client:**  
“It feels like a trapdoor. One moment I’m standing on solid ground, then it opens and I fall.”

**Clinician Note:**  
Structure identified: **Collapse**  
- Sudden downward movement  
- Loss of coherence  
- Loss of agency  
- Loss of internal access

---

## **3. Identify Forces**
Therapist:

> “What forces are acting inside this collapse?”

**Client Identifies:**  
- Pressure to perform  
- Fear of being judged  
- Internalized perfectionism  
- Old trauma around public criticism  
- High expectations from board members  
- Self-imposed standards

**Clinician Note:**  
Forces converge rapidly, overwhelming the system.

---

## **4. Locate Position**
Therapist:

> “Where are you inside this collapse?”

**Client:**  
“I’m below everything. I can’t reach my thoughts. I can’t speak. I’m just… gone.”

**Clinician Note:**  
Client is positioned **beneath** the structure — a hallmark of collapse.

---

## **5. Define Movement**
Therapist:

> “Not a solution — just movement. What would a shift look like?”

**Client:**  
“Maybe catching the trapdoor before it opens. Or having something to hold onto.”

**Clinician Note:**  
Movement = **pre‑collapse stabilization**, not post‑collapse recovery.

---

# **Part 2 — Applying V.I.T.A.L.**

## **V — Viewpoint**
**Client Viewpoint:** First‑person immersed, then *no viewpoint* during collapse  
**Shift:** Therapist invites meta‑view:

> “If you observe the collapse from above, what do you see?”

**Client:**  
“It happens fast. I don’t notice the moment before it drops.”

---

## **I — Identity**
Therapist:

> “Which identities are activated?”

**Client:**  
“The competent leader. The scared child. They’re both there.”

**Clinician Note:**  
Identity regression is part of the collapse.

---

## **T — Tension**
**Tensions Identified:**  
- Internal: competence vs. fear  
- Interpersonal: expectations vs. vulnerability  
- Structural: sudden drop  
- Temporal: past criticism trauma reactivating in present

---

## **A — Agency**
Therapist:

> “Where do you feel agency? Where does it collapse?”

**Client:**  
“I have agency before the meeting starts. Once I feel pressure, it disappears.”

**Clinician Note:**  
Agency collapses at the **moment of perceived evaluation**.

---

## **L — Landscape**
Client maps the broader landscape:  
- High-stakes nonprofit environment  
- Board scrutiny  
- Childhood history of harsh academic evaluation  
- Lack of emotional support at work  
- Chronic stress  
- Limited recovery time between meetings

**Clinician Note:**  
Landscape reveals systemic reinforcement of collapse triggers.

---

# **Part 3 — Integration**

Therapist:

> “What do you see now that you couldn’t see at the beginning?”

**Client:**  
“That the collapse isn’t random. It’s triggered by pressure and old patterns. I can work on the moment before it happens.”

---

## **Clinical Insight**
Therapist reflects:  
- Collapse is structural, not emotional weakness  
- The trapdoor metaphor reveals sudden loss of coherence  
- Identity regression drives the collapse  
- Agency restoration must occur *before* the collapse moment  
- Movement should focus on pre‑collapse detection and stabilization

---

## **Practice Adjustment**
Therapist plans to:  
- Identify pre‑collapse cues  
- Build micro‑stabilization strategies  
- Strengthen identity coherence under pressure  
- Use ISS to track collapse triggers  
- Use V.I.T.A.L. to map identity regression and tension points  
- Introduce grounding techniques at the moment of pressure

---

# **Part 4 — Training Notes for Clinicians**

### **Why this case is effective for training**
- Demonstrates ISS with a **collapse structure**, distinct from loops and push–pull  
- Shows how collapse involves loss of agency, coherence, and identity stability  
- Highlights the importance of pre‑collapse detection  
- Models how movement is defined as stabilization rather than action  
- Shows V.I.T.A.L. clarifying identity regression and tension spikes

### **How to use this in training**
- Have clinicians map the collapse visually (trapdoor metaphor)  
- Ask them to identify pre‑collapse cues  
- Have them run ISS prompts on their own collapse moments  
- Compare their own identity regressions with the client’s  
- Discuss how viewpoint shifts can prevent collapse
